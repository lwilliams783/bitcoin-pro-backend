🌍 How Markets Affect Bitcoin

Understanding the correlations between Bitcoin and traditional markets

📊 S&P 500
--
--
💡 How It Affects Bitcoin:When S&P 500 rises, it signals "risk-on" sentiment. Money flows into risk assets like stocks AND Bitcoin. When S&P falls, investors flee to cash, often selling Bitcoin too.
⚡ HIGH CORRELATION
💵 DXY (US Dollar Index)
--
--
💡 How It Affects Bitcoin:INVERSE correlation. When dollar strengthens (DXY up), Bitcoin often falls. Weak dollar (DXY down) = Bitcoin typically rises.
⚡ HIGH CORRELATION (Inverse)
🥇 Gold
--
--
💡 How It Affects Bitcoin:Both are "stores of value." When gold rises moderately ($2600-3000), it shows inflation fears = bullish for Bitcoin. Extreme gold (>$3000) often means panic = bearish.
📊 MEDIUM CORRELATION
🏢 Russell 2000 (Small Caps)
--
--
💡 How It Affects Bitcoin:Russell 2000 = ultimate "risk-on" indicator. Strong (>2500) = investors taking big risks = bullish for Bitcoin. Weak = investors play it safe = bearish.
⚡ HIGH CORRELATION
😨 VIX (Fear Index)
--
--
💡 How It Affects Bitcoin:VIX measures market fear. Low VIX (<20) = calm markets = bullish for Bitcoin. High VIX (>30) = panic = investors sell everything including Bitcoin.
⚡ HIGH CORRELATION (Inverse)

The Reverse Repo (RRPONTSYD) is where banks park cash overnight with the Federal Reserve.

💰 HIGH RRP (>$500B): Less liquidity = BEARISH for Bitcoin

🚀 LOW RRP (<$300B): More liquidity = BULLISH for Bitcoin
